            	<h3 class="sub-title">Email Marketing for NatureScapes.net</h3>
                <h4 class="sub-title-third">Background</h4>
                <p>NatureScapes is one of the longest running websites for nature photographers to network and learn from each other. As a brand, NatureScapes is comprised of several different business segments of photographers in their audience.</p>
                <p>Since 2009 I&rsquo;ve designed and developed hundreds of email communications to support various stages of the <strong>customer journey</strong>. These areas include messages for online store advertisements, luxury travel, monthly newsletters, and transactional emails throughout the website.</p>
                <h4 class="sub-title-third">Emails with Consistent Results</h4>
                <p>All emails are <strong>responsive</strong>. I have designed, developed, tested, and analyzed them from the ground up. I&rsquo;ve exceeded many benchmarks including consistently <strong>high open and click rates</strong> along with low bounce rates. Less than 10% of <a href="https://www.constantcontact.com/" target="_blank">Constant Contact</a> users accomplish this, resulting in strong <strong>customer engagement and loyalty</strong>.</p>
                <p>To increase email list sign-ups, a special offer was implemented using an autoresponder. This change resulted in a <strong>447% increase</strong> in email list growth over 6 months. In turn, the offer&rsquo;s campaign conversion rate <strong>increased by 23%</strong> with 3x the transactions in 6 months.</p>

                    <h5 class="sub-divider-title-samples">Wireframes and Outline for Responsive Template</h5>
                    <p class="page-design-title">After creating an outline of the types of email messages that are sent, I determined which elements were the most common. The goal was to create a responsive template that could be used throughout different types of communications.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="col-md-6">
                <h4 class="process-sub-title"><img src="https://www.jamieumak.com/images/gui/ico-one.svg" class="step-icn" alt="1" />Responsive Template Wireframes &amp; Design</h4>
                <p>When I first started at the company, there was not a cohesive email system in place therefore I had to build one from the ground up.</p>
                <h5 class="process-step-heading">Featured Problem: Non-existent Email System</h5>
                <ul class="list-goals">
                    <li>First, I determined what <strong>types of messages</strong> were sent for all communications in order to design and develop a core responsive template that could be used and adapted throughout. The types of emails included informational, promotional, transactional, survey invitations, autoresponders, and other notifications.</li>
                    <li>I then <strong>created an outline</strong> of each type of message and listed its <strong>purpose and goals</strong>. For example, promotional store emails contain links to featured product categories and store customer service, whereas travel ads contain links to the upcoming tour schedule and reviews.</li>
                </ul>
                <h5 class="process-step-heading">Design Approach</h5>
                <ul class="list-plain">
                    <li>For most messages, the logo, navigation, and footer were the three common elements required. I <strong>created wireframes</strong> for each section so the team could review and provide feedback. The core template layout was then <strong>designed and built</strong> with these three areas as the foundation.</li>
                    <li>Every email sent during the <strong>customer journey</strong> was designed to work together, from aquisition to purchase follow-up.</li>
                </ul>

                    <h5 class="sub-divider-title-samples">Content Creation from Website and Research</h5>
                    <p class="page-design-title">Every email design starts with a goal using research and content creation as the foundation. My favorite way to design a promotional email is to create a theme. It&rsquo;s a great way to be creative, try new approaches, and keep messages engaging.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="col-md-6">
                <h4 class="process-sub-title"><img src="https://www.jamieumak.com/images/gui/ico-two.svg" class="step-icn" alt="2" />Campaign Research &amp; Content</h4>
                <p>Email campaigns need to be designed swiftly without sacrificing quality. Using the template, campaigns are <strong>designed</strong> based on seasonal company goals. Generally, I&rsquo;m given a list of what to feature to make a content plan for the layout. At times I repurpose website <strong>content</strong>, while at other times I perform more research and write additional material to bring a <strong>unique</strong> point of view to the message.</p>
                <h5 class="process-step-heading">Harmonious Content and Visual Design</h5>
                <ul class="list-plain">
                    <li>When designing promotional messages for luxury <strong>travel</strong>, it can be <strong>challenging</strong> to create content about locations that I&rsquo;ve never visited. For inspiration, I often communicate with the company owner, read subject matter websites, and watch short YouTube videos to get an idea of what locations are like.</li>
                    <li>For <strong>eCommerce</strong> and travel promotions, I like to create <strong>themes</strong> that tie everything together as a unified message. It&rsquo;s a great way to get creative with messaging and keep things fresh rather than send the same type of advertisements repeatedly.</li>
                    <li>The final step is <strong>writing</strong> a compelling subject line with complementary pre-header text to engage the reader with the goal of increasing open rates.</li>
                </ul>

                    <p class="page-design-title">Pictured above are wireframe sketches for three different emails. Using the message content, I draw ideas and notes by hand for every email design before working on the computer. I discover the best ideas when sketching first without restriction.</p>

                <h4 class="process-sub-title"><img src="https://www.jamieumak.com/images/gui/ico-three.svg" class="step-icn" alt="3" />Wireframe Sketches &amp; Design</h4>
                <p>Once the content is complete, I always <strong>sketch wireframes</strong> by hand and mark them up with notes to incorporate into the <strong>visual design</strong>.</p>
                <h5 class="process-step-heading">Quick Idea Generation</h5>
                <ul class="list-plain">
                    <li>Sometimes I will think of additional ideas for content to feature while sketching and will go back and update the content that was written during the <strong>research</strong> phase.</li>
                    <li>After the concept is set in motion, I work on my computer and begin the visual design. During this stage I compose with supporting photography, graphics, typefaces, and colors to create a <strong>layout</strong> that is mobile friendly and will be readable on different size devices. I always design emails with the idea they are an extension of the matching landing page.</li>
                </ul>

                    <h5 class="sub-divider-title-samples">HTML Email Code, Formatted Text Version, and Email Design</h5>
                    <p class="page-design-title">When an email design is approved, the development phase begins. The campaign design is developed within the core template layout. A text only version is formatted and included for email subscribers who receive text only communications.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="col-md-6">
                <h4 class="process-sub-title"><img src="https://www.jamieumak.com/images/gui/ico-four.svg" class="step-icn" alt="4" />Development &amp; Analysis</h4>
                <p>Following the visual design of an email, it gets sent for <strong>review</strong> and changes are made if necessary. Afterwards, the design is <strong>developed</strong> as part of the responsive email template using responsive HTML and CSS, with a formatted text version to match. <strong>Testing</strong> each email in various email clients is a must for compatibility prior to deployment.</p>
                <h5 class="process-step-heading">Everything Comes Together</h5>
                <ul class="list-plain">
                    <li>Email messages are delivered based on a customer&rsquo;s preferences.</li>
                    <li>After sending I observe the <strong>performance</strong> of each campaign. UTM codes are added to all messages for campaign tracking. This is an especially helpful metric for the store, providing unique insight into what makes a <strong>successful</strong> campaign and ideas for improvement.</li>
                    <li>While there aren&rsquo;t enough resources to perform A/B testing, I instead compare similar messages and <strong>test</strong> elements like imagery, writing style, and promotion type to see how customers respond. In moving forward, <strong>iterations</strong> are made and new approaches are tested.</li>
                </ul>
